Study Overview

This experiment tests how epistemic stance framing affects advice-taking. Participants are asked to forecast a 15th-week sales point based on a 14-week chart, receive AI advice, and then update their final forecast.

The Manipulation (Between-Subjects)

The underlying math, chart curves, and advice delays (~1.5s typing speed) are strictly identical. Only the framing changes:

  • Intentional Stance (IS): Framed as an "AI Assistant" using mentalistic, first-person language (e.g., "I see a clear trend...", "I expect...").
  • Design Stance (DS): Framed as a "Forecasting Algorithm" using mechanistic, third-person language (e.g., "Pattern detected...", "The calculation indicates...").

Piloting & Error Trials

By design, the AI gives obviously bad advice on roughly 20% of the trials to test whether participants are blindly deferring or actually evaluating the chart. To help you track this during testing, error trials are currently flagged with a red banner. (This banner is strictly for debugging and will be removed before the live deployment so participants do not see it).
