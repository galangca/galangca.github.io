Carl Michael Galang

I am a second-year PhD student in the Social Brain, Body and Action Lab at McMaster University. I completed my undergraduate degree at the University of Toronto with a double major in Cognitive Science and Philosophy. My current research focus is on empathy. More specifically, I am interested in how the nervous system reacts to watching others in pain; I am also interested in how socio-cognitive factors, such as power, modulates those reactions. I use a variety of methods for my research including TMS, EMG, EEG, and behavioural responses.

I am a recipient of the SSHRC CGS-M (2015-2016), Ontario Graduate Fellowship (2016-2017), and Ontario Graduate Scholarship (2017-2018).
